Call For Posters

The ICSC 2025 poster track is a non-archival, discussion-oriented forum designed to showcase scholarly and practical contributions in social computing. We invite authors to present published or unpublished work—such as emerging ideas, prototypes and demos, datasets, systems and tools, case studies, and preliminary empirical or theoretical results—to share insights with the community and receive constructive feedback.

The objective of this track is to facilitate substantive scholarly exchange, academic reflection, and the development of future collaborations. Posters may summarize work that has appeared elsewhere, provided that the original venue is clearly cited on the poster. Because the track is non-archival, a poster presentation does not constitute prior publication and does not preclude subsequent submission to journals or conferences.

Topics of Interest

  • Advancements in methods for the broadly defined field of social computing
  • Online social network analysis, mining, and modeling
  • Large-scale social media analytics and intelligence
  • Trust, privacy, security, and fairness in social systems
  • Human–computer interaction across various domains
  • Applied social computing in areas such as health and finance
  • Governance, policy, ethical, and legal challenges of emergent social technologies
